On the 5th of August 2016, the Summer Olympic Games 2016 started. And here some facts about Rio 2016:

  1. The Summer Olympic Games 2016 took place from 5th to 21st August in Rio de Janeiro
  2. The opening and the closing ceremony took place in the Maracanã Stadium
  3. It was the first Olympic Games ever held in South America
  4. Up until today, the Summer Olympics have been held 27 times (6 editions in North America, 16 in Europe, 3 in Asia and 2 in Oceania)
  5. The first Olympic Games (Olympic Games of the antiquity) were held in 776 in Olympia (Greek) and the first Olympic Games of the modern era in 1896 in Athens (Greek)
  6. 10,500 athletes from 207 different countries took part in the Summer Olympics 2016
  7. 306 competitions in 42 different sports was held in 17 days
  8. Golf (returning after 112 years) and Rugby (returning after 92 years) became the “new” sports of the Olympic Games
  9. There will be 33 different venues in four different districts of Rio de Janeiro (Copacabana, Barra de Tijuca, Deodoro and Maracanã)
  10. A few of the football competitions was not only held in Rio de Janeiro, they was also hosted by Belo Horizonte, Brasilia, Salvador and São Paulo
  11. 7.5 million tickets were available and are already up for sale
  12. Ticket prices range from 40 Brazilian Real (10.89 Euro) to 4,600 Brazilian Real (1,252 Euro)
  13. The total costs of the Olympic Games 2016 in Rio de Janeiro will mount up to 37.6 billion Brazilian Real (10.24 billion Euro): 24.1 billion Real (6.56 billion Euro) for the infrastructure of the city, 6.5 billion Real (1.77 billion Euro) for sports facilities and 7 billion Real (1.91 billion Euro) for the operation and organization of the event
  14. Until 2016, there will be 45 events held to test the Olympic and Paralympic Games 2016
  15. In 2016, there were 8,000 employees, 85,000 third party employees and 45,000 volunteers working for the Olympic Committee Rio 2016(approximately)
  16. The slogan of the Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro is Viva sua paixão (Live your passion)
  17. The organizers prepared around 60,000 meals each day for the athletes. The regular food package will comprise rice, black beans, barbecued meat, tapioca, Pão de queijo (local cheese roll) and Açaí fruits. Cooks prepared meals during one of many food-testing exercises of the Olympic menu in Rio de Janeiro on May 6, 2016
  18. The games needed around 70,000 volunteers(from countries like the USA, U.K., Russia,China and others).
  19. With about 85,000 soldiers and police personnel, Rio 2016 saw the largest security team assembled for any event in Brazil's history. The security presence was twice as large as that for the London Olympics in 2012.
  20. The total cost of Rio 2016 is expected to be around 37.6 billion Real ($10.8 billion). This includes a city infrastructure cost of 24.1 billion Real ($6.9 billion), sports facilities of 6.5 billion Real ($1.8 billion) and an operational cost of 7 billion Real ($2 billion).
  21. The mascot for this year's Games - "Vinicius" - is named after the popular Brazilian poet and musician Vinicius de Moraes.
  22. The host city is named after a river that doesn’t exist.Legend dictates that Portuguese explorers, who landed in present day Rio in January 1502, mistook the iconic bay for the mouth of a river. They proceeded to name the area Rio de Janeiro (“River of January”), which stuck despite being wholly inaccurate.
  23. Rio was also the capital of Brazil.Some mistakenly think Rio is Brazil’s capital, but that honour actually belongs to Brasilia. It hadn’t always been that way: between 1763 and 1960, Rio was top dog (many still consider it the beating heart of Brazil).
